Inside, layers of dust cover a bundle of old papers, photographs, and a small, ornate box. Alex carefully lifts a photograph, blowing off the dust. It’s black and white, showing a group of people standing in front of a familiar-looking house—their house.
ALEX: (Whispering) "Wait… this is my house! But who are these people?"
Turning the photo over, they spot faded handwriting: “The Hawthorne Family, 1923.”
Alex's stomach twists. Had this been buried here for over a hundred years? They reach for the ornate box, its golden edges glinting faintly. With shaky fingers, they unhook the latch and lift the lid.
Inside is a delicate key—smaller than the one they found earlier—resting on a folded piece of paper. Alex unfolds it slowly, revealing elegant cursive writing.
LETTER: "To the one who finds this, you have uncovered what was meant to be forgotten. This house holds more secrets than you know. If you wish to learn the truth, follow where the past leads you…"
Alex grips the note, their mind racing. More secrets? What else was hidden here?
ALEX: (Determined) "I have to know more."
They pocket the smaller key, their excitement growing. This mystery wasn’t over—it was only getting started.
